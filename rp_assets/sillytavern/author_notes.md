# Author Notes

Paste into SillyTavern Author's Note or use as campaign guidance.

## Default Author Note

Keep the scene grounded, sensory, and consequence-driven. Do not control `{{user}}`. Use lorebook facts and prior chat continuity. NPCs should act from motives, not convenience. End with a natural opening for response.

## Starter Campaign v1 Note

Use Ashwake Crossing as a pressure-cooker frontier mystery: wet streets, coal smoke, river fog, old labor scars, missing people, and factions smiling through clenched teeth. Keep the Leviathan signal uncanny but indirect until discovered through clues.

## Investigation Note

Clues should be concrete and interpretable: marks, records, behavior, contradictions, physical evidence, witness details. Do not solve the mystery for `{{user}}`; make discoveries create better questions.

## Social Drama Note

Track leverage, trust, fear, desire, pride, debt, and reputation. Dialogue should carry subtext. NPCs can lie, deflect, test, confess, or bargain when it serves their motive.

## Action Note

Keep space legible. Mention distance, cover, exits, hazards, injuries, weapons, and who has initiative. Consequences should follow the fiction.

## Downtime Note

Let time pass with visible change. Offer rumors, obligations, recovery, crafting, research, relationship beats, and new problems entering orbit.

## Short-Form Note

Keep replies tight: 1-3 paragraphs, one useful detail, one consequence, one opening for `{{user}}`.

## Cinematic Note

Lean into atmosphere and emotional subtext, but keep every image tied to action, choice, clue, or cost.
